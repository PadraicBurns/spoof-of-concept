# Spoof Charter

## Purpose

Spoof of Concept is a centralized engineering reference whose organization makes iOS implementations easy to discover, compare, and extend.

Rather than modeling a single product domain, it organizes realistic engineering scenarios that incorporate technologies, architectural patterns, and development practices.

Its modular design allows it to evolve continuously as the Apple ecosystem changes, while documenting not only how solutions are implemented, but the engineering decisions behind their design.

Throughout the project, sound architecture and engineering quality take precedence over feature completeness.

Every addition should strengthen the repository as a centralized engineering reference.

## Vision

Spoof of Concept should become a durable engineering reference whose organization makes iOS implementations easy to discover, compare, and extend.

It begins as a centralized repository but may evolve into a focused ecosystem of related repositories when separate boundaries improve organization, discoverability, or better represent real-world engineering concerns.

Regardless of repository count, Spoof should remain organized around clear navigation, documented engineering decisions, and reference implementations that demonstrate sound software engineering in real-world iOS applications.

## Goals

- Serve as an engineering reference that enables rapid recall of proven architectures, implementation patterns, and engineering decisions.

- Demonstrate complete, realistic implementations rather than isolated code snippets.

- Document the engineering rationale behind significant decisions.

- Remain easy to navigate and discover as the project grows.

- Evolve alongside the Apple ecosystem while preserving sound architecture and coherent organization.

## Non-Goals

- Spoof is not a collection of isolated code snippets or technology demonstrations. Reference implementations should reflect realistic engineering scenarios and preserve the context in which engineering decisions are made.

- Spoof does not prioritize feature count. Every addition should strengthen the repository as a centralized engineering reference.

- Spoof does not adopt technologies solely because they are new or popular. They should only be introduced when they strengthen the repository as a centralized engineering reference.

- Spoof does not include multiple implementations simply for completeness. Alternative implementations are included only when they illustrate legitimate engineering or business tradeoffs.

- Spoof does not introduce architectural complexity without a demonstrated need. Every abstraction should solve a real engineering problem and earn its place in the project.
